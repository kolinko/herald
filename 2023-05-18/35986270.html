Mysterious Vanishing: Hackers Disappear Overnight!

By Dr. Benjamin "Nobel Scribe" Clarke - Science Columnist

Date: 23 Apr 2023

As fear and confusion grip the tech world, prominent hackers seem to be vanishing overnight. Are programming languages to blame for this sudden, unexplained disappearance? The Hacker Herald investigates.

There's chaos and confusion within the halls of Hacker Herald, as editors and journalists frantically piece together the stories of several vanished hackers. Theories are swirling, but one notion gains traction: programming languages might be at the heart of this strange phenomenon.

Our fearless Editor-In-Chief, Harvey "Clickbait" Carmichael, embraces the chaos, adding a sense of comic flare to an industry in turmoil. Amidst the panic, Dr. Benjamin "Nobel Scribe" Clarke, our esteemed Science Columnist, zeroes in on the crucial factor in this mystery: the code itself. "It's all about soil, surface, and atmosphere," he declares, as the staff scrambles to make sense of the hacker world's sudden disarray.

Soil refers to the properties of running code in a specific language, encompassing everything from performance characteristics to garbage collectors. Surface encompasses the features and quirks of the language: syntax, supported regexes, and object systems. Atmosphere involves the broader community context surrounding the language, including hiring trends and packages in repositories. The enigma of the vanished hackers might be rooted in the very core of their skills: the languages they speak.

"These conversations about programming languages seem to be fear-based and bogus," says Dr. Clarke, alluding to the absurdity of the situation. Could this hysteria be a result of programmers refusing to explore beyond their comfort zones, sticking to well-established languages, like JS, Ruby, Python, and Java? By limiting their horizons, they might be unintentionally summoning unforeseen consequences.

As the Hacker Herald searches for answers amidst the chaos, programmers are urged to examine their biases and limitations. Perhaps it's time to step out of familiar territory and give more exotic languages a chance. After all, the very future of the hacker community may depend on it.

Remember, dear reader: tech choice won't determine the success of your company, but it will shape its path.
